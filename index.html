My Story

I’ve always had a passion for arts and technology and discovered that I could mold the two into a profession that was perfect for me, which is web development. As I read more about it, the more I felt a deeper interest for this career path. The design aspect of web development is much like storytelling and the ability to create that instant spark and charm the users in such a way to want to continue to explore more is such a captivating and powerful approach to draw in the audience. Recently, I’ve found a new interest in photography and discovered another medium to express myself. I enjoy exploring cities with my kids and capturing these moments through photography. Traveling is blood in my veins and I am endlessly inspired by adventures, waking up to new horizons, and exploring the world.

What is your mission statement?

My professional mission statement is become a successful web developer. I am firm believer that company culture is everything and those that have a strong culture have higher performers. Ideally, I would love to work for company that offers flexibility (work/life balance), values integrity, appreciates dedication, and is supportive in professional development. I feel that my strong desire to learn and grow and willingness to help others is my most valuable attribute that I can bring to a company. Aside from that, I have extensive experience as a project manager, which has truly shaped me as a professional. By recognizing individual motivations and inspiring positive communication, it has helped me lead my team and our projects to success.

What sparked your interest for web development?

In today's era, the technology industry has a good scope and web development allows me to incorporate creativity, which makes the industry more professionally appealing to me. It's the combination of analytical, logical thought coupled with the creative ability to make new things and find new ways to do things that no one has done before, makes this career path exciting and promising.

What sets you apart from other new web developers?

Attitude. I am a true beliver that attitude determines direction.Although, professional skills are vital, but what good are they without the attitude to see it all through? Having the right attitude comes in many forms and is an important contribution to a company’s culture. Passion – love what you do and do what you love. With this attitude, I welcome challenges and am more determined to overcome them. Experience - It's not what you know, but it's what you DO with what you know. Respect – Let’s be honest, no one likes to work with a sour puss. Negativity sucks the energy from people. A simple act of helpfulness, kindness is such a powerful force and a little effort of this goes a long way.
